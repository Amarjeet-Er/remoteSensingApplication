Land Use And Urban Survey Division

Landuse and Urban Survey Division at Remote Sensing Applications Centre Uttar Pradesh is actively involved in formulation/creation of various types of projects/digital databases like landuse/landcover mapping, urban sprawl mapping/ monitoring.

Read More!

Training Hostels

The Centre is having fully furnished (A.C.) and well equipped Training Hostel which has been named as Shri Aryabhatt Training Hostel. It has 16 rooms, one lobby along with a fully equipped mess.

Read More!
M.Tech. in Remote Sensing and GIS

School of Geo-informatics (SGID) RSAC-U.P. Lucknow is running a two years (Four Semesters) M.Tech. course since 2013 in Remote Sensing & GIS, which is affiliated to Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam Technical University, approved by AICTE, Govt. of India.

Soil Analysis Lab

The Centre has a small soil laboratory which is equipped with basic & some advanced equipments to meet the requirement of soil analysis for ongoing projects. Some of the important parameters which can be determined in the laboratory are pH, EC, O.M., texture, CEC, soluble cations , anions & exchangeable cations and micronutrients. In addition pesticide residue can be estimated in soils.

Computer Image Processing Division

The division persistently strives to provide best in class Industry & organization aligned Information System solution. The division enables the organization to utilize the technology and information system solution for innovation, execution, inference, archiving and retrieving the information which is gleaned from plethora of Remote Sensing Satellites (Optical, Microwave), LiDAR , Bathymetry, Customized Software Development using Artificial Intelligence, MachineLearning, Deep Learning, Augmented Learning methods and other Ancillary Spatial and Non-Spatial data.

Forest Resources & Ecology Division

There is a wide range applicability of remotely sensed data in forestry including Forest Mapping and Area Estimation; Forest Density; Species Identifications; Stand Volume Estimation; Biomass Assessment; Geo-Referenced Digital Database Creation of Forest Boundaries; Mapping and Assessment of Forest Trees in Territorial Forest and Outside of Territorial Forest (TOF);

Development of Decision Support System for Sarva Shiksha Abhiyan Programme; Studies on Impact of Thermal Power, Hydroelectric Project, Mining etc. The Remote Sensing Applications Centre, U.P. Lucknow has successfully completed many studies in Uttar Pradesh showing utility of satellite data and its potential in many aspects of forest management, forest monitoring, environmental studies and wildlife habitat assessment through its Forest Resources & Ecology Division.
